Does a triangle with these side lengths exist?15, 12, 9

(A) ** Yes

(B) No

(C) I don’t know.

(D) Cannot be determined

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Triangle inequality theorem of a triangle says that the sum of the two sides of a triangle is always greater than the third side. Thus, a triangle with side lengths as of 15 units, 12 units and 9 units exist.

Concept note-2: -Which set of sides could make a right triangle? Explanation: By virtue of the Pythagorean Theorem, in a right triangle the sum of the squares of the smaller two sides equals the square of the largest side. Only 9, 12, and 15 fit this rule.

Concept note-3: -Therefore yes, it is a right triangle.

Concept note-4: -ANSWER: No; 11. SOLUTION: The sum of the lengths of any two sides of a triangle must be greater than the length of the third side.

Concept note-5: -The answer is no. There are limits on what the lengths can be. For example, the lengths 1, 2, 3 cannot make a triangle because

1 + 2 = 3

, so they would all lie on the same line. The lengths 4, 5, 10 also cannot make a triangle because

4 + 5 = 9

.

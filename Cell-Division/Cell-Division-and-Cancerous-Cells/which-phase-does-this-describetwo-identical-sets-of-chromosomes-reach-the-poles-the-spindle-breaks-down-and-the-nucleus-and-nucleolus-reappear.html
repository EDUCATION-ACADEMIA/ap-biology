CELL DIVISION

CELL DIVISION AND CANCEROUS CELLS

[SOURCES]
Which phase does this describe:Two identical sets of chromosomes reach the poles, the spindle breaks down, and the nucleus and nucleolus reappear.

(A) prophase

(B) metaphase

(C) anaphase

(D) ** telophase

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -During anaphase, sister chromatids are separated at the centromere and are pulled towards opposite poles of the cell by the mitotic spindle. During telophase, chromosomes arrive at opposite poles and unwind (uncoil) into thin strands of DNA, the spindle fibers disappear, and the nuclear membrane reappears.

Concept note-2: -During prophase, the chromosomes condense, the nucleolus disappears, and the nuclear envelope breaks down.

Concept note-3: -During telophase, the chromosomes arrive at the cell poles, the mitotic spindle disassembles, and the vesicles that contain fragments of the original nuclear membrane assemble around the two sets of chromosomes.

Concept note-4: -Telophase. The final stage of mitosis, and a reversal of many of the processes observed during prophase. The nuclear membrane reforms around the chromosomes grouped at either pole of the cell, the chromosomes uncoil and become diffuse, and the spindle fibres disappear.

Concept note-5: -During prophase, chromatin condenses into chromosomes, and the nuclear envelope (the membrane surrounding the nucleus) breaks down. In animal cells, the centrioles near the nucleus begin to separate and move to opposite poles of the cell.

EVIDENCE FOR EVOLUTION

[SOURCES]
The larvae of the common sulphur butterfly can be light green or bright yellow. Birds prey on the larvae, which are found on the green leaves of alfalfa plants. What will this butterfly population look like over the next generations?

(A) All larvae will be green

(B) All larvae will be yellow

(C) ** Most larvae will be green

(D) Most larvae will be yellow

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The essence of Darwin’s theory is that natural selection will occur if three conditions are met. These conditions, highlighted in bold above, are a struggle for existence, variation and inheritance.

Concept note-2: -Scientists often compare fossils of extinct organisms with living organisms to help determine evolutionary relationships.

Concept note-3: -Molecular similarities provide evidence for the shared ancestry of life. DNA sequence comparisons can show how different species are related.

Concept note-4: -Which of the following describes how a large mouse population with high genetic diversity will most likely be affected by a sudden, significant environmental change? Only those individuals best adapted to the new environment will survive and reproduce, leading to a decrease in genetic diversity.

Where is most of Earth’s fresh water?

(A) lakes

(B) rivers

(C) ocean

(D) ** ice caps and glaciers

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Antarctic ice sheet holds about 90 percent of the fresh water that exists on the Earth’s surface.

Concept note-2: -And, of the total freshwater, over 68 percent is locked up in ice and glaciers. Another 30 percent of freshwater is in the ground. Fresh surface-water sources, such as rivers and lakes, only constitute about 22, 300 cubic miles (93, 100 cubic kilometers), which is about 1/150th of one percent of total water.

Concept note-3: -On the planet earth most of the freshwater exists as ice caps and glaciers. Out of the remaining freshwaters, the largest proportion. A) Is found in atmosphere as moisture and clouds.

Concept note-4: -Most of Earth’s fresh water is locked in ice Most of that fresh water (68.7%) is frozen in glaciers and ice caps. Two ice sheets, the Antarctica Ice Sheet and the Greenland Ice Sheet, contain more than 99% of the ice on Earth’s surface.

Concept note-5: -Glaciers and polar ice caps store the maximum percentage of freshwater (69%), followed by ground water (30%), followed by rivers (0.33%), and lastly, the atmosphere (0.035%).
